## LinkedIn Post

In civil and construction work, a surprising amount of time disappears into small coordination checks.

Not always major design work.

Often it is things like:

- spotting what changed between revisions
- comparing quantities
- checking takeoff assumptions
- doing a quick hydraulic sanity check
- finding the right formula without digging through old notes

Those jobs matter because they sit right next to cost, delivery, and site coordination.

That is why we have added a dedicated Civil Workspace in UtilityHub.

The goal is not to create another heavy platform.

It is to give civil teams a more focused browser-side workspace for a handful of recurring tasks that are easy to underestimate but hard to avoid.

The current workspace brings together tools around:

- BOQ comparison
- takeoff and carbon estimation support
- drawing revision checks
- pressure drop and head-loss support
- civil formula lookup

We are trying to make the workspace feel narrower and more relevant, so civil users are not searching through unrelated tooling to get to what they need.

If you work in civil design, site delivery, or construction coordination, I would be genuinely interested in this:

Which task creates more avoidable friction for your team today: revision review, quantity comparison, or takeoff validation?
