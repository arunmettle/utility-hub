## LinkedIn Post

Electrical teams often work across a mix of design intent, site pressure, coordination changes, and quick technical checks.

That usually means small tasks show up constantly:

- checking voltage drop
- sizing cables
- confirming conduit fill
- sketching a panel schedule
- pulling up a formula quickly during review

None of these are unusual.

But when they happen repeatedly, they create a lot of interruption if the tooling around them feels scattered.

That is what we wanted to improve with the Electrical Workspace in UtilityHub.

We have brought together a more focused set of browser-side tools around:

- voltage drop
- cable sizing
- conduit fill
- panel schedule support
- electrical formula lookup

The intention is not to overclaim what a browser tool should do.

It is simply to make these recurring checks faster to reach, easier to use, and less dependent on jumping between tabs, spreadsheets, and old notes.

We are still shaping this with feedback, but this feels like a stronger direction than treating electrical users as just one more audience inside a generic tool list.

If you work in electrical delivery or design support, I would love to know which is most useful to improve next:

- load and sizing support
- coordination and schedule tools
- formula and reference lookup
