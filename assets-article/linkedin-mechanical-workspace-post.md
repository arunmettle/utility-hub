## LinkedIn Post

One thing I keep coming back to with engineering tools is this:

The biggest delays are not always the big decisions.

Very often, they come from the smaller review tasks around the edges:

- checking whether a revision really changed
- comparing part lists
- confirming a fit
- working through a tolerance stack quickly
- looking up a formula you know you have used before

That is the kind of work we had in mind when we put together the Mechanical Workspace in UtilityHub.

It is not meant to replace CAD, PLM, or your formal engineering systems.

It is meant to help with the everyday friction around them.

Right now the workspace brings together practical browser-side tools for:

- BOM comparison
- tolerance stack-up checks
- hole and shaft fit calculations
- drawing revision review
- recurring formula lookup

The idea is to make these tasks easier to reach and easier to use in one place, without dropping mechanical teams into a giant mixed tool directory.

We are trying to keep the experience focused, lightweight, and respectful of real working context.

If you are in mechanical design or manufacturing support, I would love to know which of these slows your team down most often:

- revision comparison
- tolerance review
- fit validation
- formula lookup
