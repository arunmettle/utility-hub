## LinkedIn Post

Over the last little while, we have been thinking about a simple question:

Are engineers really looking for "more tools"?

Usually, no.

What they actually need is a faster way to get through the small but recurring bits of delivery work that slow projects down:

- checking revisions
- comparing quantities
- validating quick calculations
- looking up formulas
- cleaning up working inputs before sharing them

That is why we have started shaping UtilityHub less like a generic utility site and more like a set of focused engineering workspaces.

We have now introduced dedicated workspaces for:

- Mechanical
- Civil
- Electrical

Each workspace is meant to feel narrower, more relevant, and more practical for the people using it.

So instead of dropping someone into a huge mixed catalog, we can now bring them into a workspace that is much closer to how they actually work.

A mechanical engineer should not have to wade through unrelated tools to get to tolerance checks, BOM comparison, fit calculations, or formula lookup.

A civil engineer should be able to get straight to BOQ comparison, takeoff support, drawing review, and hydraulic sanity checks.

An electrical engineer should be able to move quickly into voltage drop, cable sizing, conduit fill, panel schedule work, and formula lookup.

That is the direction we are leaning into now.

Still browser-based.  
Still privacy-first.  
Still focused on small tools that remove friction from real work.

This is still early, and we will keep refining it based on feedback, but it feels like a more honest and useful shape for the platform.

If you work in one of these areas, I would genuinely love to know:

What is the one repetitive engineering task you wish was easier in the browser?

Explore the workspaces here: [https://utilityhub.dev/workspaces](https://utilityhub.dev/workspaces)
